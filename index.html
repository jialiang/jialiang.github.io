About Me

Currently, I’m working as a full stack developer. Our tech team consists of seven people. We’re currently on a hybrid work arrangement.

My company promotes interior designers on our platform to homeowners looking to renovate. We have several web and mobile applications. I mainly work on the public website and our own custom ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) tool. We get millions of page views per month for our public website.

My tasks are split evenly between frontend and backend. I develop new features, fix bugs, do code reviews and help with tech-related matters.

New features are usually things to:

  • Improve conversions (e.g. website redesigns, A/B testing).
  • Improve efficiency (e.g. automation, data transformation).
  • Improve traffic (e.g. SEO improvements).
  • Improve data collection (e.g. event-based tracking, journey analytics).

Tech support involves stuff like:

  • Creating new reports in our business intelligence tool.
  • Advising on technical feasibility of ideas.
  • Setting up tools like Facebook Pixels, Google Tag Manager, Shopify and Unbounce.

We use Kanban boards to organise our tasks and work through them in order of priority. We see our task to completion independently and liaise with stakeholders as needed. For example, marketing for copy, UI designer for mock-ups, translator for translations, department heads for clarifications etc.

I mainly work with React, Redux, JavaScript, HTML, Sass and PostgreSQL. Rarely, my tasks involve MongoDB, ElasticSearch, RabbitMQ, NodeJS and Webpack. We use Git as our version control.

In my job before this, I use C#, ASP.NET, Internet Information Services (IIS) and Microsoft SQL Server. But that was a long time ago. I quit that job because working as a government contractor involves too much bureaucracy.

Currently, there’s no problem that’s causing me to leave my current company. I applied because I’m curious what other opportunities are out there. I will never mass apply for positions. I send every application after careful consideration of the job posting.

It’s not a big challenge for me to pick up new technologies. I’ve done that for various personal projects without issue.
