Senbonzakura ( Thousand Cherry Blossoms) is a song sung by virtual pop idol Hatsune Miku about the westernization of Japan during the Meiji Restoration. It was released in 2014 and has since been inducted into the Vocaloid Hall of Legends.

The scene above features visual elements found in the song's music video. All of the graphics are created by hand using SVG and CSS with a little bit of HTML5 Canvas mixed in for the cherry blossom petals. This entire webpage excluding the audio weights in at a mere 50kb when gzipped.

The major glyphs in the big spinning circle are the Twelve Earthly Branches of the Sexagenary Circle while the minor glyphs consists of Japanese Formal Numbers.

The font used for both the glyphs and this text is a semi-cursive typeface courtesy of Japanese calligrapher, Aoyagi Kouzan of the Aoni Calligraphy Classroom.
